A note on AI search before the story settled

A personal essay about AI search, and the longer argument hidden inside what gets lost when the demo becomes the argument.

I wrote this down in April 2022 after thinking about AI search from the vantage point of a coffee shop conversation that turned into an accidental seminar. The headline claim interested me less than the older question below it: what sort of world is this idea already presuming? That is often the first thing technology makes harder to see. We keep prioritizing capability, performance, and scale while deferring the question of whether the system improves daily life. AI search seemed to me like a good way to keep the older question in view.

The socially important mechanism was not the one public explanations liked to point at first. The public version usually starts with whatever can be pointed to most easily: interface, model, robot, control surface. What makes a technology socially consequential is often the second-order effect, not the headline capability. They change pacing. They alter who may stay flexible and who must start performing legibility for the machine. They change what falls through the cracks because the cracks themselves move. Once you notice that shift, AI search stop looking like a gadget and start looking like a theory of everyday life with a power source attached.

Atmosphere keeps mattering to me because it surfaces what measurement often skips. One system leaves room for human judgment. Another reshapes the environment into prompts, verifications, and small approvals. It may be difficult to quantify, though it is easy enough to feel. The tool produces not only outcomes but a certain shared feeling around those outcomes.

Looking back from April 2022, I could already feel my own position shifting. A few months earlier I had written “The problem with tool-using models is usually how novelty can impersonate depth,” and the difference between that piece and this one is instructive. That earlier note stayed closer to the surface appeal than this one does. I found myself looking past the applause toward the slower layer of maintenance, politics, sorting, and emotional obligation. The earlier optimism was not exactly wrong to my mind. It was the sort of incomplete optimism you get before the machinery has had time to reach enough real lives.

I am not trying to argue anyone into an anti-technology stance out of self-defense. The better goal is to become harder to flatter. Useful tools should invite scrutiny too, especially about the world they normalize. If that sounds like a modest ambition, good. Most of the sanest writing I know begins modestly. The healthy move is to keep the story small until reality proves it deserves to grow. That is a lesson I hear again when I reread “The problem with fragile supply chains is usually the difference between resilience and improvisation.”

A final note to myself from April 2022: the useful test is not whether a technology makes me feel early, but whether it makes the surrounding world more durable, more legible, and a little more generous. The forecast has become less interesting to me than the burden a tool lays on ordinary users. If that sounds less glamorous than prediction, good. What is glamorous is often what keeps the important part less visible.
