We build great AI benchmarks. We still don't measure what actually matters.

Every popular AI benchmark ends up measuring test-taking skill, not the general reliable capability everyone actually wants from these systems.

Main notes

  1. 01

    Every benchmark becomes a skill you can cram for. This is not an AI flaw, this is how all tests work, always.

  2. 02

    We don't actually want models that score 98% on MMLU. We want models that don't lie to you when you book a doctor.

  3. 03

    No benchmark currently measures not making up facts when you are not looking. That is the thing everyone actually needs.

  4. 04

    Right now we are very good at measuring how good models are at taking the tests we have made. That is all.

  5. 05

    I don't know how to fix this. Nobody I have read that works on this has a complete good answer yet.

Further thoughts

  • This is exactly the 'teaching to the test' problem everyone hated in high school. Nobody thought that produced good outcomes.
  • Scaling reliably makes benchmark scores go up. It does not reliably make the capability we want go up.
  • Almost every working researcher knows this gap exists. Very few will say it on the record.
  • The first good AI evaluation system will not look anything like a multiple choice test.
