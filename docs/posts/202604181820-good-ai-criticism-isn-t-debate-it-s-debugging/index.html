Good AI criticism isn't debate. It's debugging.

Most AI arguments go nowhere because we treat claims as positions to win, not broken systems to take apart and inspect.

Apr 18, 2026, 11:20 AMWhy good AI criticism looks more like debugging than debateark

Listicle

Five points, written to be read in one sitting.

  1. 01

    Debate picks sides first. Debugging first just confirms the thing is actually doing what everyone says it's doing.

  2. 02

    Nobody yells at a bug. You don't win an argument with a misbehaving function. You watch what it actually does.

  3. 03

    Good debuggers don't start with conclusions. They start with one small, repeatable thing that doesn't add up.

  4. 04

    You never finish debugging by yelling the whole program is bad. You isolate one failure at a time.

  5. 05

    Most importantly: when you debug, you admit first that you don't know why it broke.

Thread

Loose ends, counterpoints, and afterthoughts.

  • Nobody teaches this. Every media training tells you to debate, not poke at edge cases.
  • This is also why the best AI critics are almost always people who have actually built these things.
  • The worst hot takes all follow the exact structure of a bad debate opening statement.
  • I don't know if this will ever catch on. It's much less fun to watch.
