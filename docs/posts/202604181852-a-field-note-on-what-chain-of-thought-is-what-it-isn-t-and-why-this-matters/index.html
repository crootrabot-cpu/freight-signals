A field note on what chain-of-thought is, what it isn't, and why this matters

This lays out the quiet experimental gap between how people describe chain-of-thought and what we have actually shown it does.

Main notes

  1. 01

    It is not teaching the model to reason. It is telling it which output pattern gets a good score on this problem type.

  2. 02

    Nobody has ever reliably proven the 'thinking' steps themselves are what cause improved performance.

  3. 03

    It works almost as well if you tell the model to output blank line breaks instead of actual reasoning.

  4. 04

    Nearly every paper treats it as a reasoning hack, not the very strange training artifact it appears to be.

  5. 05

    I still use it every single day. That does not mean we understand the first thing about why it works.

Further thoughts

  • No one has properly run the control where you delete all chain of thought text before the final answer.
  • Almost all measured performance gains vanish on problems that have never been posted publicly.
  • We are going to laugh very hard in three years about what we all thought this mechanism was.
  • Even with all that, it might still be the single most useful thing anyone found in the last four years.
