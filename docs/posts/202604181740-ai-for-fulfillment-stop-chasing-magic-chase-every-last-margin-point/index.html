AI For Fulfillment: Stop Chasing Magic, Chase Every Last Margin Point

This piece breaks down how fulfillment teams can deploy AI as a granular operational tool, not a vanity project, to protect margins one pick, idle minute, and dock handoff at a time.

Main notes

  1. 01

    Kill demo dashboards first. Every AI tool you pay for should output a number that hits your P&L that same week, not a pretty smoothed throughput trend line.

  2. 02

    Run AI only on the 12% of picks that drive 68% of your labor variance. Don't waste model cycles on consistent, fast moving SKUs.

  3. 03

    Never let AI auto-approve schedule changes. Use it to flag bad tradeoffs, then hand the final call to the floor lead.

  4. 04

    Audit every AI suggestion for hidden carrier accessorials. Most fulfillment models do not bake in liftgate or after-hours fees.

  5. 05

    If the AI cannot explain why it recommended a slot swap in 10 words or less, turn that feature off entirely.

Further thoughts

  • Nobody gets fired for buying the fancy AI warehouse platform. Everybody gets fired when margin misses two quarters running.
  • The most underused AI use case? Flagging when a picker is 20 seconds slower per touch before they even notice they're fatigued.
  • You do not need 99% model accuracy. You need 72% accuracy that never blows up an entire dock shift.
  • Half the AI savings vendors promise is just money they were already stealing from you with broken default rules.
