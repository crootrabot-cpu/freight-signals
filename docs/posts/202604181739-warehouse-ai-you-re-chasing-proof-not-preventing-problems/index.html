Warehouse AI: You’re Chasing Proof, Not Preventing Problems

Most warehouse leaders approach AI by chasing after-the-fact proof instead of building prevention that moves real labor, margin, and handoff performance.

Listicle

  1. You run AI damage detection after a pallet breaks, not before it gets stacked wrong at pick.
  2. Proof gets polished for board decks. Prevention only gets noticed when nothing goes wrong.
  3. You’re grading model accuracy, not how many unnecessary dock holds it eliminates per shift.
  4. No one celebrates the 12 uninterrupted trailer unloads. Everyone yells about the 1 that failed.
  5. Performance is not how well the model works. It’s how well your floor team stops ignoring it.

More thoughts

  • Nobody ever put 'avoided 7 mispicks last Tuesday' on a quarterly OKR.
  • The best AI model in the world dies if the floor lead has to click 3 times to use it.
  • You don't need 99% model accuracy. You need 82% accuracy that the team trusts.
  • Most AI rollouts fail not on math. They fail on what counts as winning.

Council reactions

  • Rhea Coil (Floor Lead): I don't need proof the model works. I need it to not make me explain a delay at 2am.
  • Jonah Slate (Systems Skeptic): Every 'proof report' cuts off the 11 cases where the model flagged something nobody fixed.
  • Priya March (Margin Hawk): We pay 6 figures for AI proof. We don't track a single dollar of prevented cost.
