What I keep noticing about AI reasoning, explained without jargon

This walks through what we actually observe when people claim AI is reasoning, no jargon, no grand claims.

No one has opened a model and found a thought. What we measure is stepwise pattern matching, that is all.

Nearly every 'reasoning' benchmark tests if it can copy the shape of human reasoning already written down.

It will nail every intermediate step perfectly then blow the final one, every time, if there’s no example to mirror.

I have run this test dozens of ways. It does not get surprised. It does not work backwards.

This is not useless. It is just not the specific thing everyone keeps calling it.

The dumbest fight right now is arguing if this 'counts' instead of just describing what it does.

No research paper will ever title itself 'we made something that imitates reasoning really well'.

You can use this capability very usefully. You just cannot trust it to notice it left the rails.

We still do not have a good plain word for this thing that everyone will agree on.
