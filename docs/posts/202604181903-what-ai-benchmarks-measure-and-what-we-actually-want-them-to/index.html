What AI benchmarks measure, and what we actually want them to

This walks through the quiet gap between what benchmark scores prove about AI, and what everyone actually cares about knowing.

Every benchmark gets optimized for, eventually. Not by making the model smarter, just by making it very good at that exact test.

No one hires an AI assistant because it wins at MMLU. They hire it to not mess up their kid's field trip permission slip.

We never test for grace, or knowing when to shut up, or admitting it cannot read a messy handwritten scan.

I watched three separate teams game the same reasoning benchmark last quarter. None improved real world performance.

Worse: good benchmark scores let researchers avoid asking if the thing they built is even useful at all.

It is almost never noted how much benchmark design says far more about the academics writing them than the models.

Nobody has ever run a benchmark for how often an AI will quietly make you feel a little stupider after using it.

This gap will not be fixed by adding more test questions. It will be fixed by stopping treating evaluation like an exam.

The best model I have ever used has never appeared on any public leaderboard, for what that's worth.
