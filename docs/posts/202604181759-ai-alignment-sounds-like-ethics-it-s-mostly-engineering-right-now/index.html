AI alignment sounds like ethics. It's mostly engineering right now.

This post breaks down how the term 'alignment' has been framed as moral philosophy, while nearly all current work on it addresses narrow engineering control problems.

Main notes

  1. 01

    Nobody is currently building systems that can hold coherent moral values. No one is even close.

  2. 02

    Almost all published alignment work right now is about stopping models from outputting forbidden text strings.

  3. 03

    This is the equivalent of training a dog not to chew shoes, not teaching it right from wrong.

  4. 04

    This is not bad work. It is just not ethics. It is normal, boring reliability engineering.

  5. 05

    I don't know when or if this will shift. No one currently working on this knows either.

Further thoughts

  • It is fine that this is engineering. The lie is calling it ethics.
  • Most alignment arguments are just people arguing over which secret definition they are using.
  • If you want actual ethics work here, look at how we choose what to align systems *to*.
  • We have not had one serious public conversation about that last part yet.
