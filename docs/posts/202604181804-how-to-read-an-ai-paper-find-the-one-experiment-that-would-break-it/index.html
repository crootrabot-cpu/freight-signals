How to read an AI paper: find the one experiment that would break it

This is a Feynman-inspired method for cutting through AI research hype by focusing on the single falsifying test almost no paper runs.

Main notes

  1. 01

    Ignore the abstract graphs and opening hype. First ask only: what would prove this claim wrong?

  2. 02

    Every result has one weak hinge. For a reasoning paper, it is usually just swapping the order of answer options.

  3. 03

    Papers will run 12 variations that make them look good. They will almost never run the one that could break them.

  4. 04

    Feynman did not start reading to agree. He started by looking for the place the author avoided testing.

  5. 05

    You do not need a PhD to do this. You just need to stop reading to be impressed, and start reading to break it.

Further thoughts

  • Most peer reviewers do not do this either. That is half the field's problem right now.
  • I don't know why this is not standard practice. The break test almost always takes 1 extra hour to run.
  • The good papers I have read lately show their own breakage. You can trust those.
  • This works exactly the same way for company AI press releases.
