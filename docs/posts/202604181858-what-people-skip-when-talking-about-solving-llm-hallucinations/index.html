What people skip when talking about solving LLM hallucinations

This breaks down the unstated beliefs required to claim hallucinations are solvable, and what real evidence would shift the debate.

You first have to believe there exists a single stable correct answer for most questions people actually ask.

You need to accept factual recall is not structurally tangled up with how these models generate text at all.

You have to assume nobody will ever ask a model anything ambiguous, unproven, or contested on purpose.

You need to ignore that we cannot even reliably define a hallucination once you leave trivial fact checks.

Most of all, you have to believe we have not already hit the soft ceiling on this particular problem.

Nobody ever runs hallucination tests on the messy unscripted questions normal users actually type.

Nearly every demoed fix only performs well on the exact dataset it was tuned against.

I have yet to see a paper that tests this after ten turns of open, meandering conversation.

The quiet unspoken thing is most people do not actually want perfectly accurate answers.
