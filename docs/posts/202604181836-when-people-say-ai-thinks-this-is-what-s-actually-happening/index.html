When people say AI 'thinks': this is what's actually happening

This breaks down the mechanism behind what gets called AI reasoning, strips away marketing, and notes where we still have no good answers.

Main notes

  1. 01

    There is no little thinker inside. It does not pause, weigh, or wonder. It guesses the next most appropriate token, every single time.

  2. 02

    It can produce correct reasoning outputs the same way you can recite a math proof you memorized before you understood it.

  3. 03

    Sometimes it stumbles into correct new steps. We do not yet know reliably when this is happening, versus just pattern matching.

  4. 04

    None of this means it isn't useful. It just means "reasoning" is the wrong word for what is occurring.

  5. 05

    No one working on this actually has a full explanation. Anyone who tells you otherwise is selling something.

Further thoughts

  • The weirdest unremarked observation: it often lands the right answer before it writes out the reasoning steps.
  • We are also extremely bad at defining what human reasoning actually is, which makes this whole conversation broken at the start.
  • Every benchmark test we have built for AI reasoning can be gamed by the exact pattern matching we are trying to test past.
  • For now, the only honest answer to "does it think?" is "we can't even properly ask that question yet."
